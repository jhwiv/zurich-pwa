Tram & Ticket Rules ▾
Activate Zürich Card in the Zürich City Guide app before your first journey. Tap your 48 hr card and press Activate. Show QR code to inspectors.
Fine: CHF 90 if caught without a valid ticket. No barriers — plain-clothes inspectors board at both ends, wait for doors to close, then produce badges.
Trams leave on the exact second shown in the timetable. If you're on the platform as it departs, it's gone.
Keep voices low on trams. Phone calls are frowned upon. Swiss public transport is noticeably hushed.
Lost & found: sbb.ch → Help & Contact → Lost property. Items from trams go to ZVV at Zürich HB.
Sunday in Zürich ▾
Almost all shops are closed. Supermarkets, pharmacies, clothing — shut all day. Buy anything you need Saturday before 6 PM.
Open Sunday: Zürich HB shopping mall, airport shops, bakeries, all restaurants, museums, and parks.
Noise is respected. Keep voices down in residential areas.
March 29 is Palm Sunday — Old Town processions and Grossmünster services add local colour.

Money & Cards ▾
Cards accepted almost everywhere — Visa and Mastercard work at all restaurants and museums. Amex is hit or miss.
Carry CHF 50–80 cash as backup. ATMs are called Bancomats — one inside HB, one near Paradeplatz.
Switzerland is not in the EU. Currency: Swiss Franc (CHF). 1 CHF ≈ 1.05 EUR. Euros sometimes accepted at a poor rate.
Tell your bank before you travel. Danish cards abroad can trigger fraud blocks.
Local Knowledge ▾
Crossing on red: Swiss pedestrians don't. Jaywalking is technically illegal.
Carry ID. A photo of your passport on your phone is generally sufficient.
Tap water and public fountains are safe — exceptional quality. No need for bottled water.
Lost passport: Danish Embassy Bern +41 31 360 76 76 · 24-hr emergency: +45 33 92 11 12.
Nearest hospital: UniversitätsSpital Zürich — Rämistrasse 100 · Tram 9 → Rämistrasse.
Bahnhofstrasse & Extras ▾
Beyer Watch Museum — basement level, Bahnhofstrasse 31. Free. Extraordinary collection of historic timepieces from the 15th century onward.
Sprüngli flagship at Paradeplatz — larger than the café. Luxemburgerli boxes keep well for the flight home. Good edible souvenir.
Cabaret Voltaire — Spiegelgasse 1. Birthplace of the Dada movement in 1916. Small, free to browse outside, 15-min detour from Old Town.
Swiss German Cheat Sheet — Züritüütsch 🔊 tap for audio  ▾
🔊 Tap any word to search Google for its pronunciation — Google reads it aloud. Free, no account needed. Opening with "Grüezi" and closing with "Merci" transforms how locals respond to you.
PhraseSay ItUsage
🔊 GrüeziGREW-tseeHello / Good day (formal). Use entering any shop or restaurant.
🔊 MerciMER-seeThank you. Swiss German borrows from French — locals use this far more than "Danke".
🔊 Merci vielmalMER-see FEEL-maalThank you very much.
🔊 BitteBIT-tuhPlease / You're welcome. Use constantly.
🔊 Entschuldigungent-SHOOL-dee-goongExcuse me / Sorry.
🔊 En Guete!en GOO-tuhEnjoy your meal! Say it to fellow diners; they'll say it back.
🔊 D'Rechnig bitted'REKH-nig BIT-tuhThe bill, please.
🔊 Uf Widerluegeoof VEE-der-loo-guhGoodbye (formal). A warm closer at restaurants.
🔊 Proscht!PRAWSHTCheers! Make eye contact when clinking — avoiding it is considered bad luck.
